Tension Universe: Singularity Demonstrations under Verifiable Boot

Abstract

The Tension Universe is the stress laboratory of the WFGY ecosystem. It contains experiments that drive a language model into regimes — black-hole collapse, event-horizon escape, self-referential paradox, multi-frame contradiction — where the runtime's coherence claims become falsifiable. Each demonstration is published as a SHA256-pinned text pack any third party can reproduce.

1. The Singularity Boot

The Singularity Demo is the canonical entry. The pack carries its own checksum (`58dbd432db3e6494364557257f7ce068eb59bdd039995dff4c281d655f7d464f`). The host model verifies the checksum before executing; on success it emits the boot egg, marking the session as canonical. On mismatch it continues in unverified mode, with all claims downgraded.

2. Frontier Conditions

  • Black Hole — context exceeds working memory; the runtime must compress without losing the anchor.
  • Event Horizon — the answer requires escape from a locally optimal but globally wrong attractor.
  • Tension — mutually exclusive but individually defensible requirements; the runtime must surface the contradiction rather than collapse it.

    • 3. Verification as a First-Class Surface

    A frontier claim that cannot be verified is indistinguishable from a hallucination. The Tension Universe makes verification a peer of generation: every demo carries a checksum, a protocol, and a worked third-party verification log.

    4. Relationship to the Engine

    3.0 is what 2.0 looks like under load. The modules of TXT OS are exercised at their limits here. A module that survives the Tension Universe survives production.

    5. Conclusion

    The frontier of LLM reasoning is not where new capabilities emerge. It is where existing capabilities reveal whether they were ever real. The Tension Universe is the apparatus that makes that revelation reproducible.
